Hick's Law

Twitter

twitter.com

Hick's law states the more options a user has, the more difficult it is to use. So, the key here to a better web design is to eliminate the amount of choices. Filters are perfect for this. In the example here, Twitter does an excellent job utilising this principle. On the left of the screen, there is a list of options displayed using icons to better quickly access different functions. It makes for a more intuitive website whilst making it equally possible to access different parts of the website more quickly. It is one of the few design principles that can include multiple functions without making the design feel crowded while displaying the website owner's brand.

Fitt's Law

W3Schools

w3schools.com

It only makes sense when the time required to move to a target area--for example; clicking a button--is determined by the distance to and the size of said target area. This is Fitt's Law. W3Schools has utilised this principle rather well. Above, they have demonstrated this principle by making the search bar take up a large portion of the screen whilst placing it in the lower half of the viewport where most people's thumb can easily reach. The same can be said with the "login" button in the top right corner. The designers recognized that not everyone will have an account and therefore, login. So, they have made the button further away and smaller.

Proximity

Netflix

netflix.com

Proximity is a pretty popular and well-used design principle in web design, but I think Netflix does it exceptionaly well. The Law of Proximity exploits that innate way people group things together that are close together in space. This makes them a single perceived object. This can be a risky principle when misused or misunderstood, so careful attention is critical here. Netflix catagorizes different movie and television genres, and places the thumbnail for each movie or television show for that genre in a row. This helps signal to the viewer that they are within the same category. Therefore, proximity is mostly used to help the viewer navigate a website.
